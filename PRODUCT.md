# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users
Visitors who land on the site after seeing a photograph on social media, a link from a friend, or a search — people who appreciate images and might want one on their wall. They arrive on a phone or laptop and, if a frame moves them, wonder: *can I own a print of this?* A secondary audience is fellow photographers and friends who simply follow the work for its own sake. The job to be done: let the images do the moving, then make it easy to ask about a print of an existing photo.

## Product Purpose
A personal portfolio for a hobbyist landscape and street photographer. It showcases a curated body of already-taken work and lets a moved visitor buy a print of a photo that already exists. This is not a commercial studio: no commissions, no shoots-on-demand, no editorial services. Success is a visitor who scrolls the full gallery, feels something, and asks about a print.

## Positioning
Photographs made for the love of it, not to order. Two disciplines, one sensibility — the patience of landscape and the instinct of street, held together by warm, cinematic light. You can't book the photographer, but you can own the moment.

## Conversion & proof
- Primary CTA: "Enquire about a print" — an email to nisargi3112@gmail.com about a print of an existing photograph.
- Secondary CTA: keep scrolling the gallery / follow on social — a low-commitment path for visitors not ready to email.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: warm, cinematic photographs of wild places and city streets, and you can own a print.
- Belief ladder: (1) the images are genuinely good; (2) there's a consistent eye across landscape and street, not luck; (3) this is a real person who cares about the work; (4) asking about a print is easy and low-pressure.
- Proof on hand: the photographs themselves are the proof. Placeholder imagery is in place now; real work to be added later. No testimonials/press are claimed, since this is a personal hobby, not a business.

## Brand Personality
Warm, human, and cinematic. The voice is first-person, unhurried, and personal — someone sharing what they love, not selling a service. Dark, gallery-grade surfaces make the images glow; warmth comes from an ember accent, the writing, and the light in the photographs rather than from a bright interface. Understated, never salesy.

## Anti-references
Not a cold, techy, blue-black "cyberpunk" dark theme. Not a sterile SaaS template with identical card grids and tracked-uppercase eyebrows above every section. Not a busy stock-template photography theme with sliders, badges, and watermarks. Not a beige editorial-magazine pastiche. Not a "hire me / book a shoot" commercial studio site — this sells existing prints, not services.

## Design Principles
- Let the photograph be the design. Interface recedes; images carry the emotion.
- Warm dark, not cold dark. Warmth lives in the accent, the type, and the copy — never in a bright surface.
- One eye, two disciplines. Landscape and street should feel authored by the same person.
- For love, not for hire. The tone shares and offers; it never pitches a service or pressures a sale.
- Quiet confidence. Restraint in chrome, boldness in imagery and light.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA: body and secondary text meet contrast minimums against the dark surfaces, all imagery carries descriptive alt text, the gallery and lightbox are fully keyboard-navigable with visible focus, and every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative.
